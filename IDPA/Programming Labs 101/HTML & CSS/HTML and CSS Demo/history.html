Super Smash Bros. 64

Super Smash Bros. 64 (Smash 64) was released in 1999, but the competitive community for it was nonexistant until 2007, when netplay for it was discovered on emulators and the world started taking the game seriously. Now, the community for Smash 64 is relatively small compared two the next two installments of the series, but tournaments are still held fairly often, and Smash 64 has been featured at international tournaments.

Super Smash Bros. Melee

Super Smash Bros. Melee (Melee), was released in 2001 and was the birth of the competitive Smash Bros. community. It didn't really pick up steam until 2003, and at that point Major League Gaming (MLG) picked up Melee and ran tournaments for its pro circuit. Between 2006 - 2008 the competitive community for Melee started to fizzle, but it quickly picked back up in 2009 and has continued to grow since, spawning a documentary (which you can find a link to in the navigation menu) and re-inclusion back into MLG's circuit.

Super Smash Bros. Brawl

Super Smash Bros. Brawl (Brawl) was released in 2008 and almost killed the competitive Melee community. The game was very well-recieved by the competitive community at first, but around mid-2009 many players came to dislike it, and returned to Melee, sparking the rebirth of competitive Melee and a debate between these two games that has only just begun to fade.

The Present Community

Right now, the competitive Smash Bros. community has seen a resurgence it's never seen before. EVO, the largest Fighting Game Community (FGC) tournament, has finally accepted Smash Bros onto its roster, MLG is picking up Melee once again, and tournaments are cropping up everywhere with some of the largest turnouts the community has ever seen. Super Smash Bros. (for 3DS and Wii U, the newest installment) is coming out this year and has generated a lot of hype for players of every game in the series. At the present, 2014 is a great time to be a competitive Smash Bros. player!
